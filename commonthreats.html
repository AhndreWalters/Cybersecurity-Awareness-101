Common Cybersecurity Threats

Cyber threats come in many forms. Understanding how they work is the first step in protecting yourself online. Below are some of the most common types.

Malware

Malicious software designed to disrupt, damage, or gain unauthorized access to systems.

  1. Viruses - programs that attach to files and spread when shared.
  2. Worms - self-replicating programs that spread through networks.
  3. Ransomware - locks your files and demands payment for access.

Phishing

Attempts to trick users into giving up personal information, often through fake emails, websites, or messages.

  1. Email scams - appear to come from trusted sources.
  2. Fake websites - mimic real sites to steal logins or payment info.

Social Engineering

Exploiting human behavior to gain information rather than hacking technology.

  1. Pretexting - creating a fake scenario to trick you into giving details.
  2. Baiting - offering something tempting (like free downloads) that contains malware.

Password Attacks

Methods to crack or steal weak, reused, or stolen passwords.

  1. Brute force - guessing combinations until the correct one is found.
  2. Credential stuffing - using leaked passwords from other sites.

Public Wi-Fi Risks

Unsecured Wi-Fi networks allow attackers to intercept data.

  1. Man-in-the-middle attacks - intercepting communication between you and a site.
  2. Data theft - capturing passwords, emails, or financial info.
