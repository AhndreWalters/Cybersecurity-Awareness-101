Cybersecurity Prevention Tips

While cyber threats are constantly evolving, following basic security practices can greatly reduce your risk. These prevention tips will help you protect your information, devices, and online identity.

Strong Passwords

Use unique, complex passwords for every account. Avoid common words or personal details.

  1. Use at least 12 characters with letters, numbers, and symbols.
  2. Don’t reuse the same password across multiple accounts.
  3. Consider using a password manager.

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Add an extra layer of protection by requiring a code from your phone or email in addition to your password.

  1. Enable 2FA on all important accounts (email, banking, social media).
  2. Prefer authentication apps (like Google Authenticator) over SMS when possible.

Safe Browsing Habits

Be careful with the links you click and websites you visit.

  1. Verify sender information before opening email attachments.
  2. Check for “https://” and a lock icon in your browser before entering sensitive info.
  3. Avoid suspicious downloads and pop-ups.

Keep Software Updated

Updates fix security flaws that attackers exploit.

  1. Turn on automatic updates for operating systems and apps.
  2. Update antivirus software regularly.
  3. Replace unsupported software and devices.

Data Backups

Backups protect your files in case of ransomware, hardware failure, or accidental deletion.

  1. Back up data to an external drive or secure cloud storage.
  2. Use the 3-2-1 rule: 3 copies, 2 storage types, 1 offsite.
  3. Test your backups to ensure they work.

Public Wi-Fi Safety

Open networks can expose your activity to hackers.

  1. Avoid accessing sensitive accounts on public Wi-Fi.
  2. Use a VPN for secure browsing.
  3. Disable auto-connect for open Wi-Fi networks.

Stay Alert

Cybersecurity is not a one-time task, it’s an ongoing habit.

  1. Stay updated on common scams.
  2. Educate friends and family about safe practices.
  3. Report suspicious activity to the proper authorities.
